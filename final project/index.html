    • Albert Einstein

    One of the greatest physicists and intellectuals of all time. His contributions to theoretical physics, particularly in the areas of relativity and quantum mechanics, revolutionized our understanding of the universe

    Here's a time line of Albert Einstein life

    1. 1879 (March 14): Albert Einstein is born in Ulm, Germany, to Hermann Einstein and Pauline Koch.
    2. 1880: The Einstein family moves to Munich, Germany.
    3. 1889: Einstein's younger sister, Maja, is born.
    4. 1894: Einstein's family moves to Italy, leaving him in Munich to complete his schooling.
    5. 1895: Einstein attempts to enroll at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich but fails the entrance exam.
    6. 1896: Einstein enrolls at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich after obtaining his secondary school diploma.
    7. 1900: Einstein graduates from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic with a teaching diploma in physics and mathematics.
    8. 1902: Einstein marries Mileva Maric, a fellow student from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic.
    9. 1904: Einstein's first son, Hans Albert Einstein, is born.
    10. 1905: Einstein publishes groundbreaking papers that transform physics:
      • March: Publishes the paper on the photoelectric effect, laying the foundation for quantum theory.
      • May: Publishes the special theory of relativity, introducing the equation E=mc^2, and the theory of Brownian motion, confirming the existence of atoms.
    11. 1907: Einstein publishes a paper introducing the idea of stimulated emission of light, a key concept leading to the development of lasers.
    12. 1910: Einstein becomes a full professor at the University of Zurich.
    13. 1911: Einstein moves to Prague to become a professor at the German University of Prague.
    14. 1912: Einstein returns to Switzerland as a professor at the Eidgen√∂ssische Technische Hochschule (ETH) in Zurich.
    15. 1914: Einstein moves to Berlin to take a position at the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute and becomes a member of the Prussian Academy of Sciences.
    16. 1915: Einstein completes the general theory of relativity, proposing the concept of curved spacetime and gravitational waves.
    17. 1919: Observations of a solar eclipse confirm predictions of Einstein's general theory of relativity, making him an international celebrity.
    18. 1921: Einstein is awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
    19. 1933: Einstein emigrates to the United States to escape Nazi Germany and settles in Princeton, New Jersey, where he joins the Institute for Advanced Study.
    20. 1939: Einstein signs a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt urging research into nuclear weapons due to the potential of Nazi Germany developing them.
    21. 1940: Einstein becomes a U.S. citizen while retaining his Swiss citizenship.
    22. 1945: The atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki occur, leading Einstein to publicly advocate for nuclear disarmament.
    23. 1948: Einstein's wife, Elsa, passes away.
    24. 1952: Einstein is offered the presidency of Israel but declines.
    25. 1955 (April 18): Albert Einstein passes away at the age of 76 in Princeton, New Jersey, leaving behind a profound legacy in science and philosophy.
